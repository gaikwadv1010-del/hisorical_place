What is Raigad Fort?

Raigad Fort is a magnificent hill fort located in the Sahyadri mountain range of Maharashtra, India. Originally known as Rairi, the fort was acquired and extensively rebuilt by the legendary Maratha warrior-king Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj in the 17th century. Rising dramatically to an altitude of approximately 820 metres (2,700 feet) above sea level, it became the capital of the Maratha Empire — one of the most powerful kingdoms in Indian history.

The fort is not merely a military structure; it was a fully planned hilltop city complete with a royal palace, markets, temples, a grand court, and residential quarters. At its peak, Raigad housed thousands of people and stood as the political and administrative heart of an empire that stretched across much of the Indian subcontinent.

Location & History

Raigad Fort is situated in the Raigad district of Maharashtra, about 25 km from Mahad town and roughly 170 km south of Mumbai. The fort is accessible by a ropeway (udan khatola) or by a climb of approximately 1,450 steps carved into the mountain face. Its sheer cliffs and commanding position made it nearly impregnable to enemy attack.

The fort's origins date to around the 12th century CE, but it gained its greatest significance in 1656 when Shivaji Maharaj captured it from the Chandrarao More clan. After extensive reconstruction, it was declared the capital of the Maratha Swarajya in 1674 — the same year Shivaji was crowned Chhatrapati (Emperor) here in a grand ceremony that affirmed Hindu sovereignty in an era dominated by the Mughal Empire. After Shivaji's death in 1680, his samadhi (memorial tomb) was built within the fort complex.

"Raigad was not just a fort — it was the throne of Swarajya, the seat of a people's dream of self-rule."

— Historians on the Maratha Legacy

Historical Importance

Raigad Fort holds immense significance in Indian history as the cradle of Maratha independence. It was here that Shivaji Maharaj's coronation in June 1674 established a sovereign Hindu kingdom challenging the dominance of the Mughal Empire under Aurangzeb. This coronation was a landmark event — it gave the people of the Deccan a sense of identity, pride, and self-governance.

The fort was later captured by the Mughals in 1689 after a siege, and subsequently by the British East India Company in 1818, who dismantled many of its structures. Today, Raigad Fort is an Archaeological Survey of India (ASI) protected monument and a site of deep patriotic sentiment. Every year, lakhs of visitors and devotees climb to the fort to pay homage to Shivaji Maharaj's memory and witness the grandeur of Maratha architecture.

Interesting Facts

  • Raigad Fort has over 1,450 stone steps leading to the top; a ropeway now provides an easier ascent.
  • Shivaji Maharaj's coronation on 6 June 1674 at Raigad is celebrated annually as Rajyabhishek Sohala.
  • The fort once had 300 buildings including a palace, market street (bazaar), temple of Jagdishwar, and a grand durbar hall.
  • The Takmak Tok cliff inside the fort was historically used to throw traitors and criminals to their deaths.
  • A market road (bazaar) at the top of the fort had 24 shops for the inhabitants of this hilltop city.
  • The fort held a massive cannon called Kalyanchi Khandi, which was used as a ceremonial gun.
  • Raigad remained the Maratha capital until 1689, when Mughal general Zulfikar Khan captured it after Sambhaji Maharaj's death.
  • The Samadhi of Shivaji Maharaj located within the fort is a sacred site visited by thousands of devotees every year.
